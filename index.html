AKAN NAMES

Introduction

Akan names are derived from the Ghanian culture. Frequently in Ghana, children are given their name as a 'day name' which corresponds to the day in the week they were born; of course according to their gender. The following is a list of the Akan names that corresponds with the day they were born, according to their gender.

Male

  • Sunday: Kwasi
  • Monday: Kwadwo
  • Tuesday: Kwabena
  • Wednesday: Kwaku
  • Thursday: Yaw
  • Friday: Kofi
  • Saturday: Kwame

Female

  • Sunday: Akosua
  • Monday: Adwoa
  • Tuesday: Abenaa
  • Wednesday: Akua
  • Thursday: Yaa
  • Friday: Afua
  • Saturday: Ama

Most people know when their birthdays are, but they most likely don't know the day of the week they were born.

Hence, this application will use your information (that you will input in the form below) to calculate the exact day you were born in, and then give you your Akan name!

Fun Facts

Did you know that the popular Akan names listed above all have meanings? Here is a list of the names from both genders and their accompanying appelations. You can also read more on this topic through This Link.

  1. Akosua-Kwasi

    2. Born on a Sunday, they are believed to be naturally agile. They are quick, fast and highly-skilled in performing tasks, hence their appellation is "Agility".

  2. Adwoa-Kwadwo

    3. Born on a Monday, they like peace and avoid trouble at all costs. Their appellation means "Peaceful"

  3. Abenaa-Kwabena

    4. Born on a Tuesday, they are always friendly hence their appellation means "Friendliness".

  4. Akua-Kwau

    5. Born on a Wednesday, they are mostly associated with stubbornness, and their appellation means "Evil".

  5. Yaa-Yaw

    6. Born on a Thursday, they never care about what will result from a certain situation. Their appellation is "Bravery".

  6. Afua-Kofi

    7. Born on a Friday, They are known to be wanderers and are unable to stay in one place for long. They can travel to far places, start their own families there and never bother to check on those family members they left behind.

  7. Ama-Kwame

    8. Born on a Saturday, The males are believed to be always set for combat hence “Ready for combat” becomes his first appellation while “Snakebite herbalist” becomes his second appellation. Females are creators hence one of their appellation is “creation” while the other is “Grace”.
